🏦 1. Bancassurance
Definition

A partnership between a bank and an insurance company where the bank acts as a sales channel for insurance products to its existing customer base.

Example

You visit BRAC Bank to open an account. The officer also offers you a MetLife insurance policy. The bank earns a commission; MetLife gains a new customer — that's bancassurance.

✅

Prospects in Bangladesh

  • People trust banks → easier insurance sales
  • Expanding middle class = more customers
  • Promotes financial inclusion
  • Cost-effective distribution channel
⚠️

Challenges

  • Low public awareness of insurance
  • Bank staff need specialised training
  • Complex regulatory approvals
  • Conflict of interest risk
💱 2. Crawling Peg System
Definition

A system where a country's currency value is adjusted gradually and incrementally rather than in sudden large movements — giving businesses time to plan.

Example

1 USD = 120 BDT. Government moves it slowly: 121 → 122 → 123 BDT over months, instead of a sudden jump to 130 BDT. Exporters can plan; market panic is avoided.

💰 3. Deposit Mix
Definition

The variety/composition of a bank's deposits — savings, current, and fixed — used to balance short-term liquidity and long-term lending capacity.

💵 4. Treasury Bonds
Definition

A long-term loan you give to the Bangladesh Government. The government promises to repay with interest at fixed intervals.

Example

You buy a 10-year Bangladesh Treasury Bond worth Tk. 1 lakh. They pay you interest every 6 months and return your principal at maturity.

💶 5. Repo & Reverse Repo

📥 Repo (Bank borrows from BB)

  • Bank sells securities to BB
  • Promises to repurchase later
  • Bank gets short-term liquidity
  • BRAC Bank sells bonds to BB today, buys back tomorrow

📤 Reverse Repo (BB borrows from Banks)

  • BB buys securities from banks
  • Banks park excess funds with BB
  • Absorbs excess liquidity from market
  • BB buys bonds to inject/absorb money supply
⚙️ 6. Central Bank as Lender of Last Resort
Definition

When all commercial banks run short of funds, Bangladesh Bank provides emergency liquidity to prevent bank runs and systemic collapse.

Example

City Bank faces a sudden rush of withdrawals and runs short of cash. It borrows from Bangladesh Bank to meet demand — preventing public panic.

✍️ 7. Crossing a Cheque
Definition

Drawing two parallel lines on the face of a cheque means it cannot be cashed over the counter — it must be deposited into a bank account. This adds security.

📌 Bearer → Order Alteration is NOT Material

Even if a cheque is changed from "bearer" to "order," this is NOT a material alteration — it only adds safety. The cheque remains legally valid and the drawer must still pay.

🏦 14. Drawing Power & Power of Attorney

💳 Drawing Power (DP)

  • Maximum amount a borrower can withdraw from a loan account
  • Based on value of goods pledged to the bank
  • Bank typically lends 60–80% of pledged goods' value
  • Goods worth Tk.50 lakh pledged, bank allows 70% → DP = Tk.35 lakh

📜 Power of Attorney (POA)

  • Written authority given to someone to act on your behalf
  • Can cover banking, property, legal, business transactions
  • Must be registered for property matters
  • Businessman in Dubai gives his brother POA to handle his BRAC Bank account

🧩 23. General Banker–Customer Relationship

📌 Core Principle

When a customer opens an account, both sides enter a legal contract under the Contract Act 1872. The relationship changes direction depending on who owes whom:

  • You deposit money → you are the creditor, bank is the debtor
  • You take a loan → you are the debtor, bank is the creditor

⚖️ 25. Special Relationship — Bank's Obligations
✅

Honour Cheques

Bank must pay valid cheques if: properly drawn, sufficient balance, correct signature, presented within reasonable time.

🔒

Maintain Secrecy

Bank must keep account information private — even after account closure — except by court order or legal requirement.

🔐

Banker's Lien

Bank can retain goods or securities until dues are cleared. Two types: General Lien (any property for any dues) and Particular Lien (specific items for that specific loan).

🏦 26. Rights of a Banker
🔒

Right of Lien

Legal power to hold property or securities until debt is paid.

  • General Lien: Any property held by bank for any dues owed
  • Particular Lien: Only specific items linked to that specific loan
➕

Right of Set-Off

Allows bank to adjust a customer's credit balance in one account against debit in another.

Example

You owe Tk.1 lakh on loan but have Tk.40,000 in savings — bank can set off Tk.40,000 automatically.

🔍 39. KYC — Know Your Customer
Mandatory Under

Money Laundering Prevention Act 2012. Banks must verify identity, address, source of funds, and tax info. Required when: opening new account, adding signatory/nominee, applying for locker or new loan.

⚙️ 40. Components of a KYC Program
📋

1. Customer Acceptance Policy (CAP)

Defines which customers the bank will accept and sets risk appetite guidelines.

🆔

2. Customer Identification

Verifying identity through NID, passport, driving licence, photos, and address proof.

📊

3. Ongoing Monitoring

Continuously monitoring high-risk accounts and transactions for suspicious patterns.

📝

4. Reporting Suspicious Transactions

Filing STRs/SARs to BFIU/FIU whenever suspicious activity is detected.

🔎 41. CDD & EDD

📋 CDD — Customer Due Diligence

  • Collect & verify customer info (name, ID, address, DOB)
  • Assess money-laundering risk level
  • Rate each customer: Low / Medium / High risk
  • Applied to all customers at onboarding

🔎 EDD — Enhanced Due Diligence

  • Applied for high-risk customers and PEPs
  • Verify source of funds in detail
  • Monitor activity intensively
  • Requires senior-level approval
  • Foreign investor USD account > Tk.1 crore → EDD applies
🏛️ 42. Politically Exposed Persons (PEPs)

People with high political or official positions who pose higher risk of corruption or money laundering. All PEP accounts require EDD and continuous monitoring.

🌐

🇧🇩

Domestic PEPs (IPs)

Ministers, MPs, judges, military officials, heads of state-owned enterprises in Bangladesh

🌍

International Org Heads

Senior officials at international organisations (UN, World Bank, IMF, etc.)

⚠️ 49. Material Alteration
Definition

An unauthorised change to key terms of a negotiable instrument (amount, date, payee, etc.). It invalidates the document unless all parties consent.

📌 Note: Bearer → Order is NOT Material Alteration

Changing "bearer" to "order" only adds safety. It does NOT invalidate the cheque. The drawer must still pay.

🏦 50. Collecting Bank vs Paying Banker

🏦 Collecting Bank

  • Bank that collects cheque amount for its customer from the paying bank
  • Receives cheque, verifies, sends for clearing
  • Credits customer account on realisation

💳 Paying Banker

  • Bank on which the cheque is drawn — makes the payment
  • Verifies signature, balance, stop payment
  • Debits drawer's account
❌ 51. When Can Bank Refuse a Cheque?
  • Insufficient funds in account
  • Signature does not match specimen
  • Stop payment instruction received
  • Account frozen or stopped
  • Stale cheque (older than 6 months)
  • Post-dated (future date not yet reached)
  • Material alteration detected
  • Drawer deceased/mentally incapacitated
✅ 52. Positive Pay Instruction

💱 59. Crawling Peg — Detailed View

How it Works in Bangladesh

Bangladesh Bank adjusts the Taka exchange rate in small, predictable steps rather than sudden shocks. This gives exporters (like RMG companies) time to plan their pricing and contracts, reducing exchange rate uncertainty while still allowing the rate to reflect market realities over time.

🔐 75. Vault Cash — Dual Control
👥

Dual Control — Manager + Cash Officer

Cash is always moved in or out by Manager and Cash Officer together — never one person alone.

🔑

Dual Keys — Both Required

Both must use their own separate keys to open the safe. Never allow one person to operate both keys.

🔐 76. Safe Deposit Locker
🛡️

Safe Deposit Locker

Rented secure space with dual keys (bank key + customer key). Both keys needed to open. Nothing illegal allowed.

📜

Safe Custody

Bank keeps important documents (Wills, Title Deeds) for a fee. Sealed and stored — bank doesn't read contents.

🏆

Benefits

  • Protection from theft, fire, flood
  • Confidentiality — key holders only
  • Long-term storage for heirlooms, deeds

MODULE FBank Operations & Controls
📋 77. DCFCL — Departmental Control Function Checklist
Definition

A structured checklist ensuring bank departments follow internal control procedures. Verified daily/weekly/monthly/quarterly by branch managers. Kept for inspection by Internal Control and Senior Management.

📂 78. Storage of Accounts Records
📁

Physical Records

Keep all records in good bindings and quality files/paper. Protect from rats, insects, and moisture. Attach supporting documents properly.

💾

Digital Records

Take hard copies of computer records. Maintain backups (pen drives/secure server rooms) for disaster recovery.

🏗️ 79. Business Continuity Plan (BCP)
Definition

A plan ensuring the bank can resume operations after a disaster or cyber-attack with minimal disruption. Includes data backup, recovery processes, remote work protocols, and off-site document storage.

🧾 80. Documents & Vouchers
📄

Documents

Cheques, bills, pay orders, vouchers — either digital (IT) or paper. Each transaction is recorded with supporting source documents.

📒

Vouchers

The source document for each transaction. Shows debit and credit sides. Must be signed by accountant + supervisor. Ensures authenticity and transparency during audits.

📊 81. Daily Statement of Affairs
Purpose

A snapshot of the bank's financial position (like a mini Balance Sheet) prepared daily. Helps management track progress, identify problems, and improve strategy in real time.

📒 82. General Ledger (GL) Balancing
Definition

The GL records all assets, liabilities, equity, income, and expenses. The accounting rule: Debits = Credits for balance. Reconciliation = comparing GL with statements to ensure accuracy and catch errors.

MODULE — LAWSKey Banking Laws & Regulations
🏛️

Bangladesh Bank Order 1972

Core Functions
  • Formulate & implement monetary policy
  • Manage foreign exchange intervention
  • Maintain foreign exchange reserves
  • Regulate & supervise banking companies
  • Issue bank notes
  • Promote safe, efficient payment system
Board of Directors (Article 9)
  • Governor (Chair)
  • Deputy Governor (nominated by Bank)
  • Four Directors — non-govt officials with experience in banking/trade/commerce/industry/agriculture
🏦

Banking Company Act 1991

Section 29 — Control of Advances

Bangladesh Bank can determine lending policy for all banks, including:

  • Credit ceilings
  • Minimum small loan ratio to total advances
  • Loan purpose restrictions
  • Interest rate guidance
Section 33(2) — SLR & CRR

💰 SLR — Statutory Liquidity Ratio

  • Cash/gold/securities held by bank
  • Minimum: ≥ 13% of demand & time liabilities
  • Ensures bank can meet withdrawal demand

🏦 CRR — Cash Reserve Ratio

  • Minimum cash kept with Bangladesh Bank
  • Minimum: ≥ 5.5% of demand & time liabilities
  • Controls money supply in economy
Tier 1 & Tier 2 Capital
TierNamePurposeComponents
Tier 1Going Concern CapitalAbsorbs losses without bankruptcyPaid-up capital, reserves, retained earnings
Tier 2Gone Concern CapitalAbsorbs losses during liquidationSubordinated debts, general provisions

📌 Section 35 — Unclaimed Deposits

Deposits & valuables unclaimed for 10 years must be transferred to Bangladesh Bank.

⚠️

Definition & Consequences

A borrower who can pay but chooses not to. Consequences:

📜 MoA vs AoA

📋 Memorandum of Association (MoA)

  • Defines company's identity & external relations
  • Company name & registered office address
  • Objectives and scope of business
  • Limited liability clause
  • Share capital & subscriber details

  • Governs internal management & operations
  • Director rules, meetings, audits, accounts
  • Prospectus or statement in lieu
  • Min subscription & qualifying shares
  • Certificate of Commencement requirement
🧑 One Person Company (OPC)

📌 Key Facts

  • Introduced in 2020 amendment (effective May 2021)
  • Allows a single person to form a company with limited liability
  • Must include "One Person Company (OPC)" in its name
  • Governed by Companies Act 1994 (amended)
📊 Types of Shares
🗳️

Ordinary / Equity Share

Common ownership with full voting rights. Shareholders share in profits (dividends) and losses. Residual claimants in liquidation.

⭐

Preference Share

Priority on dividends and liquidation proceeds before ordinary shareholders.

  • Convertible: Can be converted to ordinary shares
  • Non-convertible: Stays as preference share
